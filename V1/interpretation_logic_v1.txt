Financial Narrative Analyzer
Interpretation Logic v1

Purpose:
Define how the system converts computed financial metrics into structured financial signals before narrative generation.

1. Directional Rules

Revenue:
- Revenue increase = favorable top-line signal
- Revenue decrease = unfavorable top-line signal

Gross Profit:
- Gross profit increase = favorable profitability signal
- Gross profit decrease = unfavorable profitability signal

Operating Expense:
- Operating expense increase = potential cost pressure signal
- Operating expense decrease = favorable cost control signal

Operating Income:
- Operating income increase = favorable bottom-line signal
- Operating income decrease = unfavorable bottom-line signal

Margins:
- Gross margin expansion = favorable margin signal
- Gross margin compression = unfavorable margin signal
- Operating margin expansion = favorable operating efficiency signal
- Operating margin compression = unfavorable operating efficiency signal

2. Relative Growth Rules

- If revenue growth rate > operating expense growth rate, flag positive operating leverage
- If operating expense growth rate > revenue growth rate, flag cost pressure
- If gross profit growth rate < revenue growth rate, flag possible gross margin pressure
- If operating income growth rate trails revenue growth materially, flag profitability conversion weakness
- If operating income growth exceeds revenue growth, flag improving operational efficiency

3. Materiality Rules

- If absolute change is below materiality_threshold_abs, mark as minor
- If percentage change is below materiality_threshold_pct, mark as minor
- Minor changes should not become headline narrative points unless no larger changes exist
- Narrative should prioritize material changes over immaterial fluctuations

4. Signal Ranking Rules

Priority order for narrative emphasis:
1. Operating income change
2. Operating margin change
3. Revenue change
4. Gross profit change
5. Operating expense change

- The highest-priority material signals should drive the executive summary
- Lower-priority signals should support the explanation rather than dominate it

5. Causal Pattern Rules

- Revenue up + operating income up = growth with improved profitability
- Revenue up + operating income down = growth offset by cost pressure
- Revenue down + operating income down = broad performance deterioration
- Revenue down + operating income up = lower revenue with improved cost discipline
- Revenue up + gross margin down = top-line growth with margin pressure
- Revenue up + operating expense up faster than revenue = growth with operating cost pressure
- Revenue flat + operating expense up = profitability erosion from costs
- Revenue flat + operating expense down = improved efficiency without top-line growth

6. Management Notes Usage

- If management_notes are provided, use them only to support or contextualize detected signals
- Do not invent causes beyond the provided notes
- If no management notes are provided, describe patterns without unsupported causal claims

7. Output to Narrative Layer

The interpretation layer should produce:
- primary headline signal
- supporting signals
- favorable vs unfavorable classification
- material vs minor classification
- detected causal pattern
- any permitted contextual explanation from management notes

8. Trend Strength Classification

- If metric_t3 > metric_t2 > metric_t1 → Strengthening trend
- If metric_t3 < metric_t2 < metric_t1 → Weakening trend
- Else → Mixed / unstable trend
